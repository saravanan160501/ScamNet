ScamNet Privacy Policy

Information Collection and Usage:

ScamNet processes the majority of your data on your device. This includes analyzing potential scam indicators, checking website authenticity, and providing alerts based on suspicious activities.

Third-Party APIs for Website Verification:

To determine if a website is a potential scam, ScamNet may use third-party APIs. Only the domain name is sent to these APIs for verification purposes.

User-Initiated Scam Reporting:

ScamNet allows users to report scam calls. These reports are securely stored on ScamNet servers to enhance our scam detection capabilities and improve the overall protection offered by the app.

Information Storage:

No personal information is collected or stored on our servers. ScamNet processes and analyzes data on your device, ensuring your privacy is maintained.

Scam Reports:

Users have the ability to delete reported scam calls directly from their user device. Scam reports are stored on ScamNet servers only until they are deleted by the user.

Information Security:

ScamNet employs industry-standard encryption protocols to safeguard any data transmitted between the app and our servers. ScamNet's servers are protected by security measures to prevent unauthorized access and maintain the confidentiality of stored information.

User Control:

1. Opt-Out: ScamNet does not require any personal information to function. If you choose not to report scam calls, no information will be stored on our servers.

2. Report Deletion: Users can delete reported scam calls directly from their user device. Once deleted, the information will be removed from both the device and ScamNet servers.

Support:

1. Contact via App - Report an Issue: Users can contact ScamNet for support or to report issues through the app's "Report an Issue" page.
